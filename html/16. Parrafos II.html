Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

Harry Potter was a highly unusual boy in many ways. For one thing, he hated the summer holidays more than any other time of year. For another, he really wanted to do his homework but was forced to do it in secret, in the dead of nigh. And he also happened to be a wizard.

It was nearly midnight and he was lying on his stomach in bed, the blankets drawn right over his head like a tent a flashlight in one hand and a large leather-bound book (A History of Magic by Bathilda Bagshot) propped open against the pillow.
